# Three-Phase Adoption Curve
**Source:** Novo Nordisk 3,000-person study (HBR, 2026); Michael Wade et al.
**Maps to skills:** `adoption-launch`, `ai-metrics`, `needs-guard`

---

## The Framework

AI adoption follows a predictable three-phase curve:

**Phase 1: Surge (Month 1)** — Excitement. High initial adoption. Everyone tries it. Training sessions are well-attended. Metrics look great. This is NOT real adoption — it's curiosity.

**Phase 2: Dip (Months 3-4)** — Reality hits. The tool doesn't solve everything. Edge cases frustrate. Novelty wears off. Organizations that provided one-time training see 50% churn here. This is where most AI deployments die.

**Phase 3: Rebound (Month 5+)** — Survivors find real value. Usage stabilizes at a higher, sustainable level. Advanced use cases emerge. This is real adoption.

**The critical insight:** Each phase needs DIFFERENT support:
- Phase 1: Basic training, use case discovery, enthusiasm capture
- Phase 2: Troubleshooting, advanced support, psychological safety ("it's okay to struggle")
- Phase 3: Advanced feature discovery, peer learning, integration into workflow

## Data

- Novo Nordisk trajectory: 23% (Month 1) → 15% (Month 4) → 60% (Month 8)
- Satisfaction correlates 3x more with work quality than time saved
- Organizations with phase-specific support: sustained adoption. One-time training: 50% churn.

## When to Use

Planning any AI rollout. Diagnosing adoption dip. Designing support interventions. Setting realistic adoption targets.

## When This Fails

- Mandated deployments (removes the "pull" dynamic)
- Organizations with very low digital literacy (Phase 1 may never happen)
- Crisis deployments with compressed timelines
